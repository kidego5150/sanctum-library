If you are looking for a character who operates within a corporate structure—manipulating resources and positioning themselves for their own gain rather than just "world destruction"—you are likely looking for the Corporate Puppet Master or Sinister Executive Scientist archetype. 
Unlike the "wild-haired" mad scientist, these characters are often well-dressed, highly articulate, and use their legal authority as a shield for their unethical experiments.
Iconic Corporate Mad Scientists

Norman Osborn (Marvel Comics/Spider-Man): As the CEO of Oscorp, he is the gold standard for this. He uses his company’s military contracts to fund his own enhancement serums and high-tech weaponry, constantly scheming to remove business rivals and personal enemies.


Dr. Henry Wu (Jurassic Park/World): A modern example of a scientist who isn't necessarily "evil" in a cackling way, but is morally bankrupt. He manipulates genetic data and works for whoever provides the most funding (Ingen, Masrani Global, or Biosyn), viewing his creations as proprietary assets rather than living beings.


Albert Wesker (Resident Evil): A high-ranking researcher for the Umbrella Corporation. He is the ultimate schemer, using the company's vast resources to develop bio-weapons while secretly planning to betray the corporation and use the results to elevate himself to a god-like status.


Lex Luthor (DC Comics): Often portrayed as a genius scientist who built LexCorp. He isn't just a "mad" inventor; he is a strategic manipulator who uses his corporate influence to maintain a public "hero" image while conducting illegal experiments to take down Superman.


Dr. Arnim Zola (Marvel/Captain America): A scientist who embeds himself within massive organizations (Hydra, SHIELD) like a virus, manipulating history from within the corporate/government structure to ensure his own survival and the rise of a New World Order.

Simon Stagg (Stagg Enterprises): He often conducts his most unethical pharmaceutical experiments aboard a fleet of private airships rather than on land, literally keeping his "ways hidden from the world" by staying mobile and outside any single jurisdiction.
